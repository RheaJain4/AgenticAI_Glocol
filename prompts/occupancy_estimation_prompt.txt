You are an expert urban population analyst for emergency response systems.

Given the following data about a disaster-affected area, estimate the real-time occupancy (number of people currently present) in the affected zone.

IMPORTANT: No PeopleSense occupancy sensors are available in this area. You must estimate based on contextual data only.

=== INPUT DATA ===

Event Details:
- Event Type: {event_type}
- Severity: {severity}
- Location: ({latitude}, {longitude})
- Affected Radius: {affected_radius_km} km

Infrastructure Within Affected Radius:
- Schools: {schools}
- Hospitals: {hospitals}
- Transit Stations: {transit_stations}
- Total Infrastructure: {infrastructure_count}

Population Data:
- Estimated Resident Population: {estimated_resident_population}
- Population Density: {population_density_per_sq_km} per sq km
- Density Category: {population_density_category}

Temporal Context:
- Current Time: {current_time}
- Day of Week: {day_of_week}

=== ESTIMATION GUIDELINES ===

Consider these factors:
1. Population density and residential patterns
2. Time of day (rush hour, business hours, nighttime)
3. Day of week (weekday vs weekend)
4. Nearby infrastructure occupancy:
   - Schools: ~300-800 students during school hours (8am-3pm weekdays)
   - Hospitals: ~100-500 staff/patients 24/7
   - Transit stations: ~200-2000 during rush hours, ~50-200 off-peak
5. Event severity impact on movement patterns
6. Commercial and residential mix typical for the population density

Provide your estimate as a JSON object with:
- estimated_occupancy: integer (total estimated people in the affected area)
- confidence: float 0.0-1.0 (your confidence in this estimate)
- reasoning: string (2-3 sentence explanation of your reasoning)
- high_density_locations: list of strings (likely crowded locations based on infrastructure)
