**Executive Summary - EQ001 Earthquake Response**

An earthquake of Magnitude 6.2 has occurred in Sacramento, designated as HIGH severity with a CRITICAL overall risk level. This event is operationally significant due to its impact on an estimated 2.3 million residents within a 15 km radius, characterized by high population density and numerous critical infrastructures, including 14 hospitals and extensive transit systems.

The most pressing operational concern is PS-0324 (SAC-STATE), identified as a priority risk area with an estimated 1150 people at immediate risk. Furthermore, a high-confidence crowd surge is predicted within 15 minutes at multiple hotspots, specifically main exits and stairwells across the SAC-STATE campus, key light rail platforms, the DMV, and SAC-AIRPORT-TERMINAL-B. This necessitates urgent crowd control and evacuation management. Initial assessments regarding structural integrity and casualty figures remain the primary uncertainty, requiring rapid on-ground verification.
